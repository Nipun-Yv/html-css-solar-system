Interesting Facts about Jupiter

The Great Red Spot

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has raged for at least 350 years, with winds reaching speeds of up to 432 km/h (268 mph).

Incomprehensible Size

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter of approximately 143,000 kilometers (88,846 miles), making it over 11 times wider than Earth.

It's Family of Moons

Jupiter has at least 79 moons, including Ganymede, which is the largest moon in the solar system and even bigger than the planet Mercury.

Despite its massive size, Jupiter has the shortest day of all the planets, completing one rotation in just under 10 hours.
