Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest planet in our solar system. Adorned with a dazzling system of icy rings, Saturn is unique among the planets. It is not the only planet to have rings, but none are as spectacular or as complex as Saturn's. Like fellow gas giant Jupiter, Saturn is a massive ball made mostly of hydrogen and helium. The farthest planet from Earth discovered by the unaided human eye, Saturn has been known since ancient times. The planet is named for the Roman god of agriculture and wealth, who was also the father of Jupiter.

Interesting Facts about Jupiter

The Ringed Giant

Saturn's iconic rings are made of countless small particles of ice and rock, ranging from tiny grains to massive boulders, and are divided into seven main rings.

Saturn's Day(Rotation)

A day on Saturn is very short, lasting only about 10.7 hours, due to its rapid rotation, which also causes the planet to have an oblate shape, flattening at the poles.

Titan: A Moon of Interest

Titan, Saturn's largest moon, has a thick atmosphere and surface lakes of liquid methane and ethane, making it one of the most Earth-like bodies in the solar system.

Mysterious Hexagon

Saturn's north pole features a unique hexagon-shaped storm, spanning approximately 30,000 kilometers in diameter, with winds reaching speeds of up to 322 kilometers per hour.
